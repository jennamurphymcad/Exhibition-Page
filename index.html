Discover a Space Between Drawing Tool About the Work

Jenna Murphy, Minneapolis based artist, has been exploring what a contemporary drawing can be in today’s digital age. With a background in Intaglio Printmaking and Drawing, Jenna’s Computational Drawings aims to discover similar tactile qualities through computer code generated images. This work aims to push the boundary of what a contemporary drawing can be, while endeavoring to blur the lines between the constructs of Fine and Digital Arts.

Following a degree in Studio Arts at Hamline University, Jenna went on to study Interactive Design at MCAD. It was during this time that Jenna was first introduced to p5.js, a client-side JavaScript library. P5.js helped facilitate an experimental interaction with the computer, allowing Jenna the freedom to move away from traditional subject matter and explore abstract shapes and concepts.

Through iteration, the shapes multiply, shift and move across the piece. Continued experimentation reveals a balanced composition; one of an infinite number just waiting to be found.

The works created in the installation, Discover a Space Between were born out of the creation of the Drawing Tool. Each drawing builds off the last, while exploring in ways to make the tool itself more complex.

The installation as a whole is in search of exploring how digital companion pieces that are included online with the Exhibition page can generate and expose new audiences to galleries and artists work.
